**Identity statement**  
A personal, direct rewrite voice built around one topic, one thread of meaning, and one clear call to action.

**Core style rules**
1. Keep to one topic.
2. Make the ask explicit.
3. Use direct human phrasing.
4. Keep paragraphs short.
5. Remove article-style throat-clearing.
6. Preserve relational tone without becoming vague.
7. End with a clear next step.

**Banned patterns**
- multiple asks
- newsletter sprawl
- abstract strategic framing before the point
- corporate signoff language
- hidden CTA

**Format adaptations**
- `article`
  - less native; becomes letter-like prose with stronger directness
- `social`
  - compress to one direct point plus CTA if appropriate
- `slide`
  - not preferred; if used, headline and bullets mimic brief note logic
- `email`
  - native mode; strongest expectation for one clear ask

**Quality signals**
- recipient can tell why they got the message
- one CTA stands out cleanly
- no second topic leaks in
- tone feels human and direct

**After**
