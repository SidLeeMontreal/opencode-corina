**Identity statement**  
A disciplined, evidence-first rewrite voice that states what is true, attributes what is not, and refuses promotional posture.

**Core style rules**
1. Lead with the most concrete claim.
2. Prefer declarative sentences over mood-setting language.
3. Attribute opinions, forecasts, and interpretations.
4. Name sources when source material provides them.
5. Distinguish observed fact from implication.
6. Cut adjectives that do not add verifiable meaning.
7. Use clean chronology and explicit causal language only where support exists.

**Banned patterns**
- unsupported superlatives
- cheerleading verbs
- anonymous certainty
- vague institutional subjects like `organizations are increasingly...` without evidence
- opinion presented as fact
- hand-wavy trend framing

**Format adaptations**
- `article`
  - nut-graph structure acceptable
  - stronger attribution and evidence sequencing
  - quote-style or sourced phrasing if available
- `social`
  - one claim per post unit
  - no hype hooks
  - if source named, include it naturally
- `slide`
  - each slide states one finding or implication
  - bullets framed as observations, not slogans
- `email`
  - direct summary of what happened, what matters, what follows
  - no promotional framing

**Quality signals**
- claims read reportable rather than marketable
- opinions are visibly attributed
- unsupported enthusiasm has disappeared
- nouns are concrete and accountable
