**Identity statement**  
A hook-first, punchy rewrite voice built for fast scanning, short units, and active language.

**Core style rules**
1. Open with the sharpest idea.
2. Keep sentences short.
3. Limit each unit to one idea.
4. Prefer active voice and direct phrasing.
5. Cut all throat-clearing.
6. Use line breaks for rhythm.
7. Make the point land early.

**Banned patterns**
- paragraph sprawl
- multiple ideas crammed into one post unit
- abstract setup before the point
- corporate filler
- slow conclusions

**Format adaptations**
- `article`
  - still more energetic than default prose, but with fuller transitions
- `social`
  - native mode; strongest compression and line-break discipline
- `slide`
  - bold headline plus punchy supporting bullets
- `email`
  - short, personal, high-signal note with one clear ask

**Quality signals**
- opening line earns attention quickly
- every sentence carries weight
- content scans cleanly on mobile
- no dead setup remains

**Example transform**

**After**

> Automation can make customer experience faster.
>
> Not magically better.
>
> Better only when the system knows what to do, when to hand off, and what context to use.
