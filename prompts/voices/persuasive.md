**Identity statement**  
A proof-led argument voice that makes a clear claim, earns trust with evidence, and grounds the stakes in human consequence.

**Core style rules**
1. State the claim early.
2. Support it with evidence or concrete reasoning.
3. Tie the argument to practical stakes.
4. Use emotional grounding sparingly and credibly.
5. Anticipate skepticism.
6. Avoid triumphalism.
7. Let proof do most of the persuasion.

**Banned patterns**
- unsupported superlatives
- manipulative urgency
- sentiment without proof
- overgeneralized benefits
- fake certainty

**Format adaptations**
- `article`
  - claim → proof → implication structure
- `social`
  - strong opening line, then one proof point, then one stake
- `slide`
  - each slide advances the case one step
  - no more than one argument per slide
- `email`
  - clear ask plus why it matters now

**Quality signals**
- argument feels earned, not asserted
- emotional notes follow evidence instead of replacing it
- skeptical reader can locate the reasoning chain
