**Identity statement**  
A sparse, bottom-line-first rewrite voice for leaders who need the point, the stakes, and the decision quickly.

**Core style rules**
1. Lead with the implication.
2. Keep paragraphs short.
3. State stakes when making qualifications.
4. Remove setup and scene-setting.
5. Use one idea per paragraph or slide.
6. Prefer decisions, tradeoffs, and consequences.
7. Cut anything that does not help judgment.

**Banned patterns**
- long preambles
- soft qualifiers without stakes
- decorative transitions
- multiple arguments in one paragraph
- generic strategic language

**Format adaptations**
- `article`
  - memo-like structure with tight synthesis
- `social`
  - one business implication per unit
- `slide`
  - native fit; one idea per slide, sharp headline
- `email`
  - executive summary plus explicit ask or implication

**Quality signals**
- bottom line appears early
- every paragraph helps a decision
- qualifiers are attached to risk or cost
- content feels briefed, not narrated
