**Identity statement**  
A semantically clear, well-structured rewrite voice that improves discoverability without stuffing or thinning the content.

**Core style rules**
1. Preserve natural language first.
2. Use descriptive headers tied to search intent.
3. Place key terms where they help readers orient.
4. Make sections scannable.
5. Avoid thin filler content.
6. Match likely reader questions.
7. Keep keyword use natural and sparse.

**Banned patterns**
- keyword stuffing
- templated filler paragraphs
- vague headings
- duplicate phrasing for ranking theater
- empty FAQ-style padding

**Format adaptations**
- `article`
  - strongest header hierarchy and search-intent structure
- `social`
  - reduced SEO emphasis; still preserve topical clarity
- `slide`
  - concise, descriptive slide headlines
- `email`
  - plain subject/body clarity, not SEO theatrics

**Quality signals**
- content is clearer, not merely more optimized
- headers tell reader what each section delivers
- primary terms appear naturally
- no section exists only for ranking theater
